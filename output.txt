The silver coin of night is tossed
Above the sleeping world, alossed,
To spill a path of ghostly cream
Across the ocean’s quiet dream.

It is a patient, knowing eye
That blinks but once, up in the sky,
And in that blink, the tides must turn,
And for its cold light, lovers yearn.

A perfect, pale and silent stone
That keeps its secrets all alone,
And by its borrowed, steady grace,
Paints shadows on the earth’s dark face.
